About Kerala

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is renowned for its lush landscapes, vibrant culture, and rich history. Often referred to as "God's Own Country," the state is famous for its stunning backwaters, serene beaches, and verdant hill stations. Kerala's unique geographical features include a network of interconnected canals and lagoons, making it a popular destination for houseboat cruises. The state is also known for its diverse wildlife, with numerous national parks and wildlife sanctuaries. Culturally, Kerala boasts a blend of various influences, reflected in its festivals, traditional arts, and cuisine. The state's rich heritage includes classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, as well as a thriving film industry.

History

Kerala has been inhabited for thousands of years. Its spices attracted traders from the Roman Empire, Greece, and Arabia, making it an important trade center. From around the 1st century CE, the Chera dynasty ruled Kerala. They promoted trade and cultural exchange. In the late 15th century, Portuguese explorers, including Vasco da Gama, arrived, marking the start of European influence. Following the Portuguese, the Dutch and then the British took control. Today, Kerala is celebrated for its cultural diversity and natural beauty.
